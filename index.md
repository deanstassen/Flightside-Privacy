# FlightHUD Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 10 July 2026**

FlightHUD is a simple flight-tracking HUD app made by an independent developer (Dean
Stassen). This policy explains what the app does and doesn't do with your data. It's
short because the app doesn't do very much with data — that's deliberate.

## The short version

FlightHUD does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to me or to any third
party. Everything the app shows you — speed, altitude, position — is read from your
device's GPS and displayed on your screen. It never leaves your phone.

## What the app accesses, and why

**Location (GPS).** FlightHUD uses your device's location services to show live speed,
altitude, heading, and position. This is the core function of the app — without it,
there's nothing to show. Your location is read on-device and used only to update the
display in real time. It is not recorded, logged, uploaded, or shared with me or with
any third party. Nothing is sent to a server, because the app doesn't have one.

**Settings and routes you enter.** If you choose your preferred units (knots vs mph vs
km/h, feet vs metres) or manually enter a flight route (an airport pair or a position/
bearing), that information is saved only in your device's local app storage, so it's
remembered next time you open the app. It stays on your device and is deleted
automatically if you delete the app. I never see it.

**Map imagery.** The map view is powered by Apple Maps (MapKit), built into iOS. To
draw the map, Apple's own map service receives the map area you're viewing, which may
correlate with your location — this works the same way it does in any app that shows a
map, including Apple's own Maps app. That exchange happens directly between your device
and Apple, under Apple's privacy policy, not mine: see
[Apple's Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/) and
[Apple Maps & Privacy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/data/en/maps/). I don't
operate any map servers and never see this data.

## What the app does not do

- No account or sign-up, so there's no personal profile to create or store
- No analytics, crash reporting, or third-party tracking SDKs of any kind
- No advertising, and no data shared with advertisers
- No data sold to anyone, ever
- No data collected from children — the app isn't directed at children and doesn't
  knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13 (or the
  relevant age in your region)

## Your data, your rights

Because FlightHUD doesn't collect or hold any personal data, there's generally nothing
for me to give you a copy of, correct, or delete — it never left your device in the
first place. If you ever have a question about this, or believe that's not accurate,
email me at [dean@flightside.io](mailto:dean@flightside.io) and I'll look into it.

## Future changes

If a future version of FlightHUD adds a feature that does transmit data — for example,
an optional flight-number lookup that needs to reach a server — this policy will be
updated first, with a new "last updated" date at the top, before that feature ships.
The version of the app you're using may differ from what's described here if you
haven't updated recently; check this page for the current version.
